Brug af modalverber

Lige så forskellige modalverberne er fra andre engelske verber, når vi taler om grammatik (infinitiv, do-omskrivning, kongruens, perfektum participium etc.), lige så forskellige er de fra andre verber, når vi taler om brug af modalverberne.

Intet modalverbum betyder én og kun én ting. De har alle flere betydninger, og to eller flere af ordene kan betyde næsten det samme. Der er ofte kun tale om en gradsforskel i betydningen.

Fælles for dem er, at vi bruger dem, når vi vil udtrykke vores holdning til, om noget vil ske:

• om det er nødvendigt, (you must go now)
• om det er sandsynligt, (he will be here this afternoon)
• om det er muligt, (I might make it for tomorrow)
• om det er ønskværdigt. (John should marry her)

Evne: can, could.

He can play football.
He could read before he was five.

Tilladelse: can, could, may.

Can we leave now?
Could we leave now? (mere høfligt)
May we leave now? (mest høfligt, respektfuldt)
You can go whenever you want to.
You may leave when you have finished your papers.

Mulighed: can, could, may, might, must.

He can be right.
He could be right.
He may be right.
He might be right. (men det er ikke særlig sandsynligt)
You must have seen that car. (det kan ikke passe andet)
We might come to the party next Saturday. (måske, måske ikke)

Ønske: ought to, shall, should, will, would, must.

You should not give her that book. (det er en dårlig ide)
I think you should marry her. (et godt råd)
You ought to marry her. (du kan ikke være andet bekendt)
Will you come with me to the dance next Saturday? (det vil jeg gerne, at du vil)
Would you care for a drink? (har du lyst til det?)

Vilje: will, would, must.

I will not accept that question. (det kan der ikke være tale om)
I would never buy her that dress. (uanset hvad der måtte ske)
You must be here Friday at the latest. (det insisterer jeg på)
You must never play that song again. (det forbyder jeg)

NB! Når man udtrykker sin vilje i forhold til et eller andet, kan man oftest vælge at bruge det almindelige verbum have to i stedet for et modalverbum.

You must be here on Friday./You have to be here on Friday.

Nødvendighed: must.

In Denmark, children must have nine years' education. (det siger loven)
People must eat to survive. (ellers dør de)

NB! Som ved vilje kan man også ved nødvendighed vælge at bruge det almindelige verbum have to i stedet for et modalverbum; i dette tilfælde must.

In Denmark, children have to go to school./In Denmark, children must go to school.
People have to eat to survive./People must eat to survive.
